Problems from Chapter 4 of Statistical Rethinking.

Problem 4E1

The first line: \(y_i \sim \text{Normal}(\mu,\sigma)\).

Problem 4E2

Two parameters, \(\mu\) and \(\sigma\).

Problem 4E3

\[ \text{Pr}(\mu,\sigma|y) = \frac{\text{Normal}(y|\mu,\sigma)\text{Normal}(\mu|0,10)\text{Exponential}(\sigma|1)}{\int\int\text{Normal}(y|\mu,\sigma)\text{Normal}(\mu|0,10)\text{Exponential}(\sigma|1)\mathrm{d} \mu \mathrm{d} \sigma} \]

Problem 4E4

The second line: \(\mu_i = \alpha + \beta x_i\).

Problem 4E5

Three: \(\alpha\), \(\beta\), and \(\sigma\). Note that \(\mu_i\) is not an independent parameter since it is a deterministic function of \(\alpha\), \(\beta\), and \(x_i\).

Problem 4M3

\[\begin{align} y_i &\sim \text{Normal}(\mu_i,\sigma) \\ \mu_i &= \alpha + \beta x_i \\ \alpha &\sim \text{Normal}(0, 10) \\ \beta &\sim \text{Uniform}(0, 1) \\ \sigma &\sim \text{Exponential}(1) \end{align}\]

Problem 4M4

\[\begin{align} h_{ij} &\sim \text{Normal}(\mu_{ij},\sigma) \\ \mu_{ij} &= \alpha_j + \beta x_i \\ \alpha_j &\sim \text{Normal}(80, 10) \\ \beta &\sim \text{Normal}(0, 10) \\ \sigma &\sim \text{Exponential}(1) \end{align}\]

\(i \in \{-1, 0, 1\}\) indexes years, \(j \in \{1,\ldots,n\}\) indexes students. \(\alpha_j\) is the mean height (in cm) of students in the age rage considered, and \(\beta\) is growth in cm/year.

Problem 4M5

A log-Gaussian prior for \(\beta\) is more appropriate. Lets assume a prior mean growth of 8 cm/year with a standard deviation of 4 cm/year (i.e., a variance of 16 cm\(^2\)/year\(^2\)). Looking up the relevant quantities for the log-Gaussian distribution, we find:

\[\begin{align} 8 &= \exp \left(\mu + \frac{\sigma^2}{2}\right) \\ 16 &= \left( \exp \sigma^2 - 1 \right) \exp\left(2 \mu + \sigma^2 \right) \end{align}\]

After some algebra (which a computer can do for you), we get

\[\begin{align} \mu &= 4 \ln 2 - \frac{1}{2} \ln 5 \approx 1.97 \\ \sigma^2 &= \ln 5 - 2 \ln 2 \approx 0.223 \end{align}\]

With \(\sigma \approx0.472\), we choose as our prior for \(\beta\)

\[ \beta \sim \text{Log-Normal}(1.97, 0.472) \] Lets do some simulation and see whether we like our prior.

betas <- rlnorm(1e4, 1.97, 0.472)

ggplot(data.frame(betas), aes(betas)) +
    geom_density() +
    geom_function(fun = dlnorm, args = list(meanlog = 1.97, sdlog = 0.472), colour = "red") +
    labs(x = expression(beta),
         y = "density")

That looks fine.

Problem 4M6

The simplest way to take account of the fact the variance of heights never goes beyond 64 (meaning the standard deviation never goes beyond 8) is to use

\[ \sigma \sim \text{Uniform}(0, 8) \]
